Frequently Asked Questions

The binomial distribution is a discrete probability distribution that models the number of successes in a fixed number of independent trials, each with the same probability of success.

  • Fixed number of trials (n)
  • Each trial has only two outcomes: success or failure
  • Probability of success (p) is the same for all trials
  • The trials are independent of each other

Flipping a coin 10 times to count the number of heads is a binomial experiment because each flip is independent, has two outcomes (head or tail), and has a fixed probability of success (p = 0.5).
